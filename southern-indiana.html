Southern Indiana

Fun Stops

If you travel down I65 from Indianapolis, you will find yourself in Columbus, Indiana. Columbus is the home of Cummins engines. You can enjoy their small museum as well as some very interesting architecture throughout the town. Enjoy a real old-fashioned soda fountain and calliope in Zaharako’s downtown. Across the street, your children will enjoy the wonderful multi-story indoor play area in the Commons.

Southern State Parks

Clifty Falls State Park is filled with natural beauty and great trails as well as a nice inn. Charlestown State Park is a former federal munition manufacturing site with boat ramp access to the Ohio River. A wonderful trail here takes you among the ruins of Rose Island, a former, riverside amusement park destroyed by a massive flood in 1937.

Natural Wonders

Marengo Cave, a natural wonder with easy cave tours, has cave tours where the visitor can crawl using a headlamp. Hemlock Cliffs National Scenic Trail is exciting, as you hike and negotiate interesting obstacles. Jug Rock is the largest rock formation in the state. Spring Mill State Park trails lead to creeks that appear to come out of rocks and an historic village that shows life in the 19th century.

National History

Lincoln Pioneer Village is a restoration of a town in Lincoln’s day. There are log cabins and a nice museum. Lincoln State Park has historical sites and trails Lincoln must have taken. Lincoln Boyhood National Historical Park occupies a portion of the Lincoln family 100-acre farm. The cabin is no longer standing; a foundation replica shows where it stood.
