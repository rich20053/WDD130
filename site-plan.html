Overview

Purpose

To increase revenue by creating an online presence that will provide information about our river adventures to local people as well as others who may be planning to visit our areal. This website will also allow users to become familiar with our many offerings as they make their vacation plans. In addition, it will allow them to make to reservations for their equipment and for guides, if desired. It will include a mechanism that will accept online payment for trips, gear, and services they would like to take. The website will synchronize with our current point of sale and reservation systems.

Audience

This website will be for individuals and families that enjoy or want to try rafting adventures on the river. They will likely be users would be people planning an adventure for themselves or for their families. They would have a wide range of skill levels, from novice to expert. They would be the type of person who loves the outdoors and loves adventure and excitement. They might be someone who needs and adrenaline rush or someone looking for a fun way to spend some time with their family. They want this adventure to be easily planned, easily executed and reasonably priced. They want to make some memories. They are people who use cell phones or have some other online access.

Typography

Heading Font: Georgia

I find this to be a strong, standrad serif font that I think will draw the user's attention. It should work on a lot of machines.

Paragraph Font: Verdana Pro

I chose this font because I think it is a clear sans serif font that will be easy to read and will contrast with the heading font.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
